How to Find the Error Details

Using Browser Developer Tools

If your error happened while clicking a button or submitting a form inside a system, follow these steps to capture the exact details. This helps the IT team pinpoint the problem faster.

  1. 1
    Open Developer Tools

    Go back to the page where the error happened. Press F12 on your keyboard — a panel will appear on the side or bottom of your screen.

  2. 2
    Click the Network tab

    At the top of the panel that opened, click the tab labeled Network.

  3. 3
    Repeat the action that caused the error

    With the Network tab visible, do the same thing again — click the same button, fill in the same form, or perform the same steps that triggered the error.

  4. 4
    Find the failed request

    A list of entries will appear. Look for one highlighted in red, or one showing a number like 400, 422, or 500 in the Status column. Click on it.

  5. 5
    Open the Payload or Form Data section

    In the details area that appears, look for a tab called Payload, Form Data, or Request Body. Select all the text there and copy it.

  6. 6
    Paste it in the field

    Come back to this form and paste what you copied into the User Input / Payload field.

Can't find the Network tab or this doesn't match your browser? That's okay — just skip this field and describe what happened in the Problem Description instead.
